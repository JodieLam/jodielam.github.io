Interns Australia Website


Redesigning for clarity

The Interns Australia website is imperative for the organisation to provide resources and support to interns and employers. Their existing website was inconsistent and overloaded with information, making it difficult for their users to find information. To mitigate this, I worked closely with the team to rebrand and redesign the website so the navigation was clear, and the content was useful and up to date. 

https://internsaustralia.org.au/ 


 The Process 

  1. ANALYSIS & RESEARCH - Customer interviews were conducted to understand their requirements and the issues they were currently experiencing. A current state UX review of the website was also done to identify their needs and pain points.
  2. SYNTHESIS - Data from the initial analysis and interviews were consolidated through a thematic analysis. This assisted with identifying the key requirements for the website and to recommend changes to mitigate their pain points. 
  3. PROTOTYPE - High and lo-fi prototypes were created initially on paper and Figma. These were iteratively designed and developed with the team based on their feedback. 
  4. PUBLICATION - Once the team was aligned and satisfied, the designs were developed and published onto Wordpress. Later on, due to changes in strategy and costs, the website was migrated and redeveloped on Mobirise.

Project Challenges

Changing Requirements

Towards the end of the project, the organisation was downsizing and needed to reduce costs. This required changes to the content and the design of the website to reflect the changing circumstances. Additionally, a cheaper CMS Mobirise was sourced to publish the website. 

Lack of Design Guidelines

There wasn't a pre-existing design system which led to the disorganised branding of the website. A design guideline was created for all their digital assets, and was applied on the website to ensure the colours, typography and copy was consistent.

Working Remotely

The team works remotely across different states. This made some of the interviews, prototyping and testing challenging as it had to be done virtually, instead of face to face. This was mitigated by planning meetings early in advanced and using video conferencing tools.

 The Final Solution 

After conducting customer research and joint prototyping with the team, the final Interns Australia website was published using the Mobirise CMS with a brand consistent with the devised design guidelines. The content has been refreshed so it is up to date and is aligned to the current services of the organisation.
