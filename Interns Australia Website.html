Project Summary

The Objective

The Interns Australia website provides resources and support for students and employers to ensure the internship landscape in Australia is equitable and beneficial to everyone. 

The Problem

Visitors had issues finding information as the content was out of date and scattered across the website. The web hosting costs were also high, which was unsustainable for a voluntary not-for-profit business.

The Solution

The content, information architecture and front end was redesigned to reflect the current nature of the organisation. This was done through customer interviews and co-creating with the team.

The Process

Customer Research & UX Review

Customer interviews were conducted via Google Meet to identify the requirements and issues they were experiencing. A current state UI/UX review of the website was also done to identify the pain points.

Data Analysis & Synthesis

Data from the initial analysis and interviews were consolidated through a thematic analysis. This assisted with identifying the key requirements for the website and to recommend changes to mitigate their pain points.

Prototyping and Content Creation

High and lo-fi prototypes were created initially on paper. These were iteratively designed and developed with the team based on their feedback. New copy and content was also created based on the review.

Testing & Implementation

Once the design was finalised, the new website design was tested with the team to ensure all requirements were satisfied. Upon approval, the website was  implemented using Filezilla and through the new CMS Mobirise.
